Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 26, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes the data practices of LazyMatch, a mobile puzzle game published by TheLazyDevs Inc. (“we,” “us,” or “our”). We believe your privacy is a right, not an option, so we have designed LazyMatch to work without collecting any personal information whatsoever.

🔒 Information We Collect

We collect no personal data. None.

LazyMatch does not collect, store off-device, transmit, or share any personal or sensitive information. The only data the app ever touches is your game progress (level completions, scores), and that data is saved exclusively on your own device — it never leaves it.

📱 Android Permissions

LazyMatch requests no Android permissions. It does not ask for access to your camera, microphone, contacts, location, storage, or any other device capability. You will not see a permission prompt when installing or running the app.

🌐 Network Access & Third Parties

LazyMatch makes no network or internet requests. The app functions entirely offline and contains no third-party SDKs, no advertising networks, no analytics or telemetry libraries, and no in-app purchase infrastructure.

LazyMatch is distributed through Google Play. Google may collect certain information as part of operating the Play Store platform (such as download statistics). That data is governed by Google’s Privacy Policy , not ours.

👤 Children’s Privacy

LazyMatch is a general-audience game rated suitable for ages 13 and older. It is not directed at or primarily designed for children under the age of 13. Because we collect no data from anyone, we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under 16 where a higher age threshold applies).

If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us at lazydevsinc@gmail.com and we will take appropriate steps — though, given that the app collects nothing at all, there is nothing for us to delete.

🔓 Data Security

Your game progress is stored using the standard local save mechanism provided by Android. Because that data never leaves your device and is never transmitted anywhere, the risk of a data breach involving LazyMatch is effectively zero. The security of your device itself is governed by your device manufacturer and Android.

📝 Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we ever add features that involve collecting personal data — such as advertisements, analytics, online leaderboards, or in-app purchases — we will update this policy before those features ship, so that it always accurately reflects current practice.

The “Last updated” date at the top of this page will reflect any revision. Continued use of LazyMatch after a policy change constitutes acceptance of the updated terms.
